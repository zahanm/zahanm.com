San Francisco My $0.02

This is just a starter list of places I could think of that I find fun visiting. I've grouped them into bits that fit together, ie you could do one group in a day. Let me know if you want an explanation of particular ones. I've deliberately left out tourist traps I don't think are worth anyone's time, like Fisherman's Wharf.

This section is places towards the North of the city, mostly pretty sights focused.

Palace of Fine Arts
Built for the World Fair. Looks neo-classical and slightly out of place.
Crissy Field
Take a kite and prepare to be blown away.
Golden Gate
You know it.
Hawk Hill
To see the bridge properly, I'd recommend driving over it from the city side to the north, and then up to Hawk Hill around sunset. Lesser know to tourists.
Tiburon
Tiburon is about 20 min drive from the bridge. It's a pretty town. Have dinner at Don Antonio's, a divine Italian place (pricey), get the gnocchi.

North Eastern corner of the city.

North Beach
Known for Italians
Washington Square
This park is a good home base and there's a great pizzeria next to it, Tony's.
Beach Blanket Babylon
It's a musical / play said to be funny (lots of American humor).
Ferry Building
Humprey Slowcombe ice cream now has a branch here. But the lines are round the block.
Coit Tower
Great views of the city.

Mid-city exploration.

Alamo Square Park
This park is the one you see in postcards. The Victorian houses on a slated gradient with he financial district in the background. Very picturesque.
Exploratorium
The E is a science museum. This one focuses on hands on physical sciences demonstrations (compare to CalAcademy).
Top of the Mark
A bar with cool views of the city at night.

Must at least drive through the park.

Golden Gate Park
There's a conservatory of flowers and botanical garden. The two museums are in the middle.
CalAcademy
California Academy of Sciences is natural sciences, think full rainforest habitat in a glass dome and living roof.
De Young Museum
De Young is art, can't say I'm a fan.
Ocean Beach
It's a good ending to this, get drinks at Cliff House.

Very off beat for tourists, but these are gems in the North West.

Inner Richmond
This is where Asian people actually live, no they don't live in Chinatown. Great Asian food.
Asian Art Museum
AA museum is great if their temporary exhibit is one you're interested in. Check up beforehand.
Legion of Honor
This is classical art, like the Greeks.
Lands End
Nice short walk. View of bridge.

Dunno if you'll be around for this, but if you want to experience "SF", you have to be here for a festival. Luckily the biggest one is around the corner. Prepare to watch the whole city celebrate in the Pride Parade on June 29, 2014.
